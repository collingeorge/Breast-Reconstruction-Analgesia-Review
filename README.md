## Overview

This repository contains three independent educational literature reviews examining perioperative care in breast reconstruction revision surgery from distinct analytical perspectives: analgesic strategy, patient communication, and recovery measurement.

Each document is a narrative educational review of published literature prepared as part of an independent academic portfolio project. None of the documents constitute clinical protocols, institutional guidelines, order sets, or decision-support tools. The author is a pre-medical applicant and not a licensed physician or other healthcare professional.

## Intellectual Architecture

The three documents examine distinct but related dimensions of the same perioperative setting:

- **02** addresses what the published literature says about analgesic strategy and its measured outcomes
- **03** addresses how communication quality shapes whether patients can meaningfully participate in those decisions
- **04** addresses whether the outcome constructs used to evaluate analgesic strategies adequately capture patient-experienced recovery

Each document has an independent analytical contribution. Together they form a coherent scholarly portfolio rather than a collection of independent pieces.

## Interpretive Caution

The literature reviewed in these documents spans heterogeneous clinical populations, institutional practices, anesthetic techniques, and evidence quality levels. Each document is a narrative educational review, not a systematic review or formal meta-analysis. No PRISMA framework was applied, and no formal evidence-grading methodology was used.

Findings discussed across these documents reflect the published literature and should not be read as validated clinical guidance, performance targets, or prescriptive standards of any kind.

## Intended Use

This repository is appropriate for:

- Scholarly review of perioperative analgesic literature in breast reconstruction revision
- Educational discussion of patient communication and informed consent in perioperative settings

- Independent academic portfolio presentation

## Evidence Base

The three documents collectively draw on published literature including ERAS Society guidelines, ASRA practice advisories, ASA/APSF safety communications, randomized trials and systematic reviews on opioid-sparing anesthesia and regional techniques, patient communication and health literacy research, shared decision-making literature, and perioperative recovery measurement science.

Full references with DOIs are provided in `06_References.md`.
